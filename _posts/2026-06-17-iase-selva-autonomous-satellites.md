## 🛰 活動簡介｜About the Seminar

本次專題演講將探討地球觀測太空系統在資料數量、品質與時效性需求提高後，衛星與衛星星系任務在設計與運行上所面臨的複雜度挑戰。

Dr. Daniel Selva 將介紹其團隊在提升太空載具設計與運行自主性方面的最新研究進展，內容包含強化學習、多目標優化、自主感測網路，以及去中心化市場機制任務分配演算法等主題，並討論這些技術如何提升地球觀測任務的回應能力與效能。

This seminar will discuss how Earth observation space systems have become increasingly complex as requirements for data quantity, quality, and timeliness continue to grow.

Dr. Daniel Selva will present recent progress toward increasing autonomy in spacecraft design and operations, including reinforcement learning, multi-objective optimization, autonomous sensor webs, and decentralized market-based task allocation algorithms for Earth observation task scheduling.

## 🧠 專題演講摘要｜Abstract

隨著地球觀測任務對資料數量、品質與時效性的要求日益嚴格，太空系統的複雜度已快速增加。單一太空載具的複雜度持續提升，而現行與規劃中的系統也愈發依賴大型衛星星系系統及其他分散式架構。這在兩個層面上帶來挑戰：一是如何設計此類複雜系統，二是如何有效運行與管理這些系統。

同時，生成式 AI 與基礎模型的快速發展正在改變工程領域，並自動化大量任務，也讓一些人認為，衛星與衛星星系系統等複雜系統的端到端自動化設計與運行時代即將到來。

在本次研討會中，Dr. Daniel Selva 將概述其團隊近期在提升太空載具設計與運行自主性方面的研究進展。首先，他將展示強化學習代理（reinforcement learning agents）如何學習設計合成策略，並在太空載具設計任務中超越多目標優化演算法。接著，他將介紹其團隊為自主感測網路（autonomous sensor webs）所建立的自主性技術堆疊，並展示新開發的去中心化市場機制任務分配演算法，如何在地球觀測任務排程問題上優於現有最先進的集中式與去中心化方法。

這些能力最終有望改變太空任務的構思與運行方式，大幅縮短開發週期，並提升地球觀測系統的回應能力與效能。

The complexity of space systems for Earth observation has rapidly increased due to ever more stringent requirements on the quantity, quality, and timeliness of the datasets required. Individual spacecraft complexity has grown, and current and planned systems rely on large constellations and other distributed architectures. This poses challenges at two levels: designing such complex systems and operating them.

At the same time, extremely rapid advances in generative AI and foundation models have revolutionized engineering, automating many tasks and leading some to believe that the days of end-to-end automated design and operations of complex systems such as satellites and constellations are near.

In this seminar, Dr. Daniel Selva will provide an overview of his team’s recent progress toward pushing the level of autonomy in both spacecraft design and operations. First, he will show that reinforcement learning agents can learn design synthesis policies that can outperform multi-objective optimization algorithms in spacecraft design tasks. Then, he will discuss their autonomy stack for autonomous sensor webs and show how their new decentralized market-based task allocation algorithms outperform state-of-the-art centralized and decentralized methods in Earth observation task scheduling problems.

Ultimately, these capabilities could transform how space missions are conceived and operated, dramatically reducing development timelines while increasing the responsiveness and effectiveness of Earth observation systems.

## 👨‍🏫 講者簡介｜Speaker Bio

**Dr. Daniel Selva** 現任美國德州農工大學（Texas A&M University）航太工程系副教授，並主持 Systems Engineering, Autonomy, and Knowledge representation（SEAK）Lab。他的研究聚焦於複雜系統設計與運行中的演算法決策制定與決策支援系統，並特別著重於太空系統相關應用。

Dr. Selva 於 2004 年取得巴塞隆納加泰隆尼亞理工大學（Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya）與法國 ISAE-SUPAERO 的電機工程與航太工程雙學位 BS+MEng，並於 2012 年取得麻省理工學院（MIT）太空系統博士學位。2004 至 2008 年間，他曾於法屬圭亞那庫魯（Kourou）擔任 Ariane 5 發射團隊的航電專家，參與並貢獻於 21 次成功發射任務。加入 Texas A&M University 之前，他於 2014 至 2018 年任教於 Cornell University，擔任助理教授。

Dr. Selva 已共同發表超過 100 篇同儕審查論文，並獲得多項榮譽，包括 2023 Texas A&M Engineering Young Faculty Fellow Award 與 2025 College of Engineering Teaching Impact Award。他也是 AIAA Associate Fellow、*Journal of Aerospace Information Systems* 副編輯，以及 AIAA Intelligent Systems Technical Committee 的候任主席。

**Dr. Daniel Selva** is an Associate Professor of Aerospace Engineering at Texas A&M University, where he directs the Systems Engineering, Autonomy, and Knowledge representation（SEAK）Lab. His research focuses on algorithmic decision making and decision support systems for the design and operation of complex systems, with a strong emphasis on space systems.

Dr. Selva received dual BS+MEng degrees in electrical and aerospace engineering from Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya in Barcelona and ISAE-SUPAERO in Toulouse in 2004, and a PhD in Space Systems from MIT in 2012. Between 2004 and 2008, he worked as an avionics specialist on the Ariane 5 launch team in Kourou, French Guiana, contributing to 21 successful launches. Before joining Texas A&M University, he was an Assistant Professor at Cornell University from 2014 to 2018.

Dr. Selva has co-authored over 100 peer-reviewed publications and received multiple honors, including the 2023 Texas A&M Engineering Young Faculty Fellow Award and the 2025 College of Engineering Teaching Impact Award. He is an AIAA Associate Fellow, an Associate Editor for the *Journal of Aerospace Information Systems*, and the Chair Elect for the AIAA Intelligent Systems Technical Committee.
